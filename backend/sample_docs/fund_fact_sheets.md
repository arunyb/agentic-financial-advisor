# Sample Fund Fact Sheets (Synthetic, for demo purposes only)

Global Total Market Equity Index Fund (Ticker: GTMX) seeks to track the performance of a broad, globally diversified index of large- and mid-capitalization stocks across developed and emerging markets. Historical synthetic data shows an average annual return of roughly 8.5% with a standard deviation near 15%, an expense ratio of 0.05%, and no minimum holding period, making it a common core holding in growth-oriented portfolios.

Aggregate Bond Index Fund (Ticker: AGBX) invests in a broad mix of investment-grade government and corporate bonds across short, intermediate, and long durations. Synthetic historical data shows an average annual return near 4.2% with a standard deviation around 5%, an expense ratio of 0.04%, and it is typically used to dampen overall portfolio volatility and provide income.

Short-Term Treasury Fund (Ticker: STTX) holds government securities maturing in one to three years. It has synthetic historical volatility below 2% and an average annual return around 3.1%, making it appropriate for capital preservation and near-term liquidity needs rather than growth.

Emerging Markets Equity Fund (Ticker: EMEX) invests in equities from developing economies and carries higher expected volatility, with synthetic standard deviation near 22% and average annual return near 9.5%. It is generally suggested only as a smaller satellite position for investors with longer time horizons and higher risk tolerance.

Real Estate Investment Trust Fund (Ticker: REIX) provides exposure to a diversified basket of publicly traded real estate companies. Synthetic data shows an average annual return near 7% with a standard deviation around 17%, and it is often used for diversification benefits and income generation through dividends.

Money Market Fund (Ticker: MMFX) invests in high-quality, short-term instruments and aims to preserve principal with minimal volatility, generating a synthetic yield around 4.5% in the current sample rate environment. It is commonly used for cash-equivalent holdings and short-term goals.
