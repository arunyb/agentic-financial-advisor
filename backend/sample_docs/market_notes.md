# Sample Market Notes (Synthetic, for demo purposes only)

Model allocation guidance for a conservative investor typically favors capital preservation, often weighting bonds and cash equivalents around 65-75% of the portfolio, with the remainder in diversified equities to help offset inflation over time.

Model allocation guidance for a moderate investor typically targets a balanced mix, often close to 55-65% equities and the remainder in bonds and cash, aiming to balance long-term growth against manageable short-term swings.

Model allocation guidance for an aggressive investor with a long time horizon often favors growth, with equity allocations frequently in the 80-95% range and a smaller allocation to bonds or cash reserved mainly for rebalancing flexibility and short-term liquidity needs.

General best practice in portfolio construction suggests reviewing asset allocation at least annually, or after a major life event, and rebalancing when any asset class drifts more than roughly five percentage points from its target weight.

Concentration risk arises when a portfolio holds an outsized position in a single stock, sector, or geography. A widely cited rule of thumb suggests limiting any single non-index equity position to a modest percentage of total portfolio value to reduce idiosyncratic risk.

Inflation erodes the purchasing power of cash and low-yielding fixed income over time, which is why most long-horizon financial plans maintain a meaningful allocation to growth assets such as equities even for otherwise risk-averse investors.
